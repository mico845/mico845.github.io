1 The Kinematics of Mass Points

1.1 Reference frames

the chosen object as a reference of the motion.

1.2 Mass point

Mass is also called Particle.
If we may ignore its size and shape, we may therefore regard the object as a single point with a mass(mass point).
Treating an object as a mass point is conditional and relative. So, we must make specific analysis for specific cases.

1.3 Position vector

vector →\rightarrow→ direction, magnitude
scalar →\rightarrow→ magnitude

AND ∣ a→ ∣|~\overrightarrow{a}~|∣ a ∣ is aaa,
then dot product is a scalar

\begin{align*} \overrightarrow{A} \cdot \overrightarrow{B} = AB \cos \alpha \end{align*}

cross product is a vector

\begin{align*} |\overrightarrow{A} \times \overrightarrow{B}| &= AB \sin \alpha\\ \overrightarrow{A} \times \overrightarrow{B} &= (AB \sin \alpha)\overrightarrow{e} \end{align*}

Let position vector r→=x i→+y j→+z k→\overrightarrow{r} = x~\overrightarrow{i}+y~\overrightarrow{j}+z~\overrightarrow{k}r=x i+y j​+z k,
then magnitude ∣r→∣=x2+y2+z2|\overrightarrow{r}| = \sqrt{x^{2} + y^{2} + z^{2}}∣r∣=x2+y2+z2​,

direction:
cos⁡α=x∣r→∣, cos⁡β=y∣r→∣, cos⁡γ=z∣r→∣\cos \alpha =\frac{x}{|\overrightarrow{r}|},~\cos \beta =\frac{y}{|\overrightarrow{r}|},~\cos \gamma =\frac{z}{|\overrightarrow{r}|}cosα=∣r∣x​, cosβ=∣r∣y​, cosγ=∣r∣z​

1.4 Equation of motion

\begin{align*} \overrightarrow{r}~(t) = x(t)~\overrightarrow{i}+y(t)~\overrightarrow{j}+z(t)~\overrightarrow{k} \end{align*}

the trajectory equation can be obtain by eliminating ttt.

displacement Δr→\Delta \overrightarrow{r}Δr

\begin{align*} \overrightarrow{r_{A}}&= x_{A}~\overrightarrow{i} + y_{A}~\overrightarrow{j}\\ \overrightarrow{r_{B}}&= x_{B}~\overrightarrow{i} + y_{B}~\overrightarrow{j}\\ \Delta \overrightarrow{r} &= \overrightarrow{r_{B}} - \overrightarrow{r_{A}} = (x_{B}-x_{A})~\overrightarrow{i}+(y_{B}-y_{A})~\overrightarrow{j} \end{align*}

3-D motion:

\begin{align*} \Delta \overrightarrow{r} &= (x_{B}-x_{A})~\overrightarrow{i}+(y_{B}-y_{A})~\overrightarrow{j} + (z_{B}-z_{A})~\overrightarrow{k} \end{align*}

notice

\begin{align*} \Delta \overrightarrow{r} &= \overrightarrow{r_{2}} - \overrightarrow{r_{1}}\\ |~\Delta \overrightarrow{r} ~|&= |\overrightarrow{r_{2}} - \overrightarrow{r_{1}}|\\ \Delta r &= r_{2} - r_{1} = |\overrightarrow{r_{2}}| - |\overrightarrow{r_{1}}| \end{align*}

path Δs\Delta sΔs

\begin{align*} \Delta s = \mathop{P_{1}P_{2}}\limits^{\frown} \end{align*}

difference

  1. Displacement is unique
  2. Generally ∣Δr→∣≠Δs|\Delta \overrightarrow{r}| \neq \Delta s∣Δr∣​=Δs
  3. In the limit as ∣Δt→0|\Delta t \rightarrow 0∣Δt→0, ∣ d r→ ∣=∣ ds ∣|~d~\overrightarrow{r}~|=|~ds~|∣ d r ∣=∣ ds ∣
  4. Displacement is a vector, path is a scalar.

1.5 Velocity

average velocity

\begin{align*} \Delta \overrightarrow{r} &= \overrightarrow{r}~(t + \Delta t) - \overrightarrow{r}~(t)\\ &= \Delta x~\overrightarrow{i} + \Delta y~\overrightarrow{j}\\ \overline{~\overrightarrow{v}~} &= \frac{\Delta \overrightarrow{r}}{\Delta t} \\ & = \frac{\Delta x}{\Delta t}~\overrightarrow{i} + \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta t}~\overrightarrow{j}\\ & = \overline{v_{x}}~\overrightarrow{i} + \overline{v_{y}}~\overrightarrow{j} \end{align*}

instantaneous velocity

\begin{align*} \overrightarrow{v} &= \lim \limits_{\Delta t \to 0}{\frac{\Delta \overrightarrow{r}}{\Delta t}} = \frac{d~\overrightarrow{r}}{dt}\\ &= \frac{dx}{dt}~\overrightarrow{i} + \frac{dy}{dt}~\overrightarrow{j}\\ &= v_{x} ~\overrightarrow{i} + v_{y} ~\overrightarrow{j} \end{align*}

As Δt→0\Delta t \to 0Δt→0, ∣ d r→ ∣=ds|~d~\overrightarrow{r}~| =ds∣ d r ∣=ds

\begin{align*} \overrightarrow{v} &= \frac{ds}{dt} ~\overrightarrow{e_{t}} \end{align*}

direction: tangent to trajectory a that point
magnitude: v=dsdtv = \frac{ds}{dt}v=dtds​ (the magnitude of velocity equals to speed)

speed
average speed

\begin{align*} \overline{v} &= \frac{\Delta s}{\Delta t} \end{align*}

instantaneous speed

\begin{align*} v &= \lim \limits_{\Delta t \to 0}{\frac{\Delta s}{\Delta t}} = \frac{ds}{dt} \end{align*}

Yeah, the magnitude of instantaneous velocity equals to instantaneous speed.
