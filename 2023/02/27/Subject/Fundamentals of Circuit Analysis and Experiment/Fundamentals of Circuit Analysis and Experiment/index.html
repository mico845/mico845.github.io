1 Basic Concepts

Electric circuit theory and electromagnetic theory are the two fundamental theories upon which all branches of electrical engineering are built.

An electric circuit is an interconnection of electrical elements.

By the analysis of a circuit, we mean a study of the behavior of the circuit:

  1. How does it respond to a given input?

  2. How do the interconnected elements and devices in the circuit interact?

1.1 Charge

Charge is an electrical property of the atomic particles of which matter consists, measured in coulombs( CCC ).
electronic charge e=1.602×10−19Ce=1.602 \times 10^{-19}Ce=1.602×10−19C (the charges that occur in nature are integral multiples of the electronic charge)

1.2 Current

Electric current is the time rate of change of charge, measured in amperes( AAA ).

\begin{align*} i \triangleq \frac{dq}{dt} \end{align*}

So, the unit of ampere can be derived as 1A=1C/s1 A = 1C / s1A=1C/s.

  • A direct current (dc) flows only in one direction and can be constant or time varying.
  • An alternating current (ac) is a current that changes direction with respect to time.

actual direction of current is the direction of positive charge moving.
reference direction of current can be defined artificially.

If the reference direction is the same as the actual current direction, the current value is positive; if not, the current value is negative.

1.3 Voltage

Voltage(or potential difference) is the energy required to move a unit charge through an element(from a reference point to another point), measured in volts( VVV ).

\begin{align*} v_{ab} &= \frac{dw}{dq}~~~(volt) \end{align*}

1.4 Power

Power is the time rare of expending or absorbing energy, measured in watts( WWW ).

\begin{align*} p = \frac{dw}{dt}=\frac{dw}{dq} \cdot \frac{dq}{dt} &= vi \end{align*}

The power absorbed( pabp_{ab}pab​ ) or supplied( −pab-p_{ab}−pab​ ) by an element is the product of the voltage across the element and current through it.

Current direction and voltage polarity play an major role in determining the sign of the power.

Sign conventions
Passive sign convention is satisfied when the current enters through the positive terminal of an element and pab=vip_{ab} = vipab​=vi.

Active sign convention is satisfied when the current enters through the negative terminal of an element and pab=−vip_{ab} = -vipab​=−vi.

Positive sign implies that the element is absorbing power.
Negative sign implies that the element is releasing or supplying power.

\begin{align*} + Power~ ~absorbed &= - Power~ ~supplied\\ \end{align*}

Because of the Law of Conservation of Energy,

\begin{align*} \sum p = 0 \end{align*}

2.2 Nodes, Branches and Loops

branch
A branch represents a single element such as a voltage source or a resistor.

node
A node is the point of connection between two or more branches.

loop
A loop is any closed path in a circuit.
A loop is said to be independent if it contains at least one branch which is not a part of any other independent loop.
Let independent loops lll, branches bbb, nodes nnn,
then we obtain l=b−n+1l = b-n+1l=b−n+1.

Mesh
A mesh is a loop that does not contain other branches and nodes.
