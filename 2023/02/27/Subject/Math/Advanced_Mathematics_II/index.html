1.1 Vectors in plane and in space

1.1.1 Vectors

Certain quantities in nature process both a magnitude and a direction, and they are called vectors, aka, a vector is a directed line segment, denoted by $\overrightarrow{a}$.

If $\overrightarrow{a}$ and $\overrightarrow{b}$ have the same length and direction, they are equal( $\overrightarrow{a} = \overrightarrow{b}$ ).

zero vector, denoted by $\overrightarrow{0}$, is with any direction.

the length, or magnitude, of a vector $\overrightarrow{a}$ is frequently denoted by $|a|$ and is always a non-negative number.

A unit vector is a vector of length $1$. If $\overrightarrow{a} \neq 0$, then $\overrightarrow{a} = |\overrightarrow{a}|$ is a unit vector in the direction of $\overrightarrow{a}$.

Two nonzero vector $\overrightarrow{a}$ and $\overrightarrow{b}$ are said to be parallel or collinear, denoted by $\overrightarrow{a} // \overrightarrow{b}$ , if they have the same or opposite directions.
And for any vector $\overrightarrow{a}$ , we see that $\overrightarrow{0}~// ~\overrightarrow{a}$ since the direction of the zero vector is arbitrary.

Two nonzero vector $\overrightarrow{a}$ and $\overrightarrow{b}$ are said to be orthogonal or perpendicular, denoted by $\overrightarrow{a} \bot \overrightarrow{b}$.

The vector $\overrightarrow{a{1}}, \overrightarrow{a{2}},\dotsb,\overrightarrow{a_{k}},~(k \geq 3)$, are called coplanar if when their initial points are moved to the same point, their terminal points, together with the common initial point, lie in the same plane.
