1.1.1 Vectors

Certain quantities in nature process both a magnitude and a direction, and they are called vectors, aka, a vector is a directed line segment, denoted by a→\overrightarrow{a}a.

If a→\overrightarrow{a}a and b→\overrightarrow{b}b have the same length and direction, they are equal( a→=b→\overrightarrow{a} = \overrightarrow{b}a=b ).

zero vector, denoted by 0→\overrightarrow{0}0, is with any direction.

the length, or magnitude, of a vector a→\overrightarrow{a}a is frequently denoted by ∣a∣|a|∣a∣ and is always a non-negative number.

A unit vector is a vector of length 111. If a→≠0\overrightarrow{a} \neq 0a​=0, then a→=∣a→∣\overrightarrow{a} = |\overrightarrow{a}|a=∣a∣ is a unit vector in the direction of a→\overrightarrow{a}a.

Two nonzero vector a→\overrightarrow{a}a and b→\overrightarrow{b}b are said to be parallel or collinear, denoted by a→//b→\overrightarrow{a} // \overrightarrow{b}a//b , if they have the same or opposite directions.
And for any vector a→\overrightarrow{a}a , we see that 0→ // a→\overrightarrow{0}~// ~\overrightarrow{a}0 // a since the direction of the zero vector is arbitrary.

Two nonzero vector a→\overrightarrow{a}a and b→\overrightarrow{b}b are said to be orthogonal or perpendicular, denoted by a→⊥b→\overrightarrow{a} \bot \overrightarrow{b}a⊥b.

The vector a1→,a2→,⋯ ,ak→, (k≥3)\overrightarrow{a_{1}}, \overrightarrow{a_{2}},\dotsb,\overrightarrow{a_{k}},~(k \geq 3)a1​​,a2​​,⋯,ak​​, (k≥3), are called coplanar if when their initial points are moved to the same point, their terminal points, together with the common initial point, lie in the same plane.
